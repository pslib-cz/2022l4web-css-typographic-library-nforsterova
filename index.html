Headings <h1> - <h6>

The <h1> to <h6> HTML elements represent six levels of section headings. <h1>> is the highest section level and <h6>> is the lowest. You use them to struture your document.

Lists <ol> <ul> <li>

Ordered list

The <ol> HTML element represents an ordered list of items This list is typically rendered as a numbered list. His items are <li>. As a example we have easy pancakes recipe here.

  1. Sift the dry ingredients together.
    1. Flour
    2. Baking powder
    3. Sugar
    4. Salt
  2. Make a well, then add the wet ingredients. Stir to combine.
  3. Scoop the batter onto a hot griddle or pan.
  4. Cook for two to three minutes, then flip.
  5. Continue cooking until brown on both sides.

Unordered list

The <ul> HTML element represents an unordered list of items, it is typically a bulleted list. His items are <li>, same as in ordered lists.

  • Flour
    • all-purpose flour
  • Baking powder
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Milk and butter
  • Egg
